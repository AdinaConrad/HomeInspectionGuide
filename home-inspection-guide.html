the first things to go in any older home
📅
These show up on almost every inspection. They're not deal-breakers — but they will need replacing. Knowing the lifespans going in protects you from surprises later.
🌡️
Hot Water Heater
Most common appliance finding on any inspection. Over 10 years old? Plan for it. Replacement: $800–$1,800 installed. If it's past its window, ask for a credit.
Lifespan: 10–15 years
❄️
AC Unit
Old units run — until they don't, usually on the hottest August day. Get a quote now if it's aging. Central AC replacement: $4,000–$8,000+.
Lifespan: 15–20 years
🫧
Washer & Dryer
Included in many sales and often overlooked. Older units are on borrowed time. Budget $1,200–$2,500 for a new pair if needed.
Lifespan: 10–13 years
🔥
Furnace / Boiler
If it's working it's usually fine to proceed — but know the age. Replacement: $3,000–$6,000. Factor this into your total cost picture.
Lifespan: 15–25 years
🍽️
Dishwasher & Refrigerator
Rarely flagged, always aging. If the home includes older appliances, factor in turnover — especially if you're already stretched on purchase price.
Lifespan: 10–15 years

🐚 a few things worth saying out loud
📄
a long report is not a red flag
Inspectors are paid to find things — a detailed report on an older home means the inspector did their job. It doesn't mean the home is falling apart.
🔢
decisions come from numbers, not feelings
When something big shows up, the next step is a contractor quote — not a gut reaction. Once you have a real number, you can make a real decision.
🤝
you will never be pushed toward a home you don't want
This process is yours. If the report genuinely changes how you feel about a home, that is completely valid. The right home is still out there. We'll find it.
